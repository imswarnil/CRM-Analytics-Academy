# Connecting & Syncing Data

Every dataset starts with raw data. In CRM Analytics, that data arrives through **connections** and is pulled in by **syncs**. This lesson covers how to get clean, scheduled data flowing into the platform.

## Connections vs. syncs

Two related concepts often get confused:

- A **connection** is a defined link to a source — a Salesforce org, an external database, Snowflake, an S3 bucket, or a CSV upload.
- A **sync** (sometimes called a *connector*) actually copies selected objects and fields from that connection into CRM Analytics on a schedule.

You configure both in the **Data Manager**.

## Local vs. external connections

CRM Analytics distinguishes where data comes from:

- **Local Salesforce connection** — your own org's objects (Accounts, Opportunities, custom objects). Always available, no extra setup.
- **External connections** — other Salesforce orgs or third-party systems, configured with credentials and an output connector.

::note
Start with the local Salesforce connection. Most first projects only need standard objects like Opportunity, Account, and User.
::

## Choosing fields to sync

When you add a Salesforce object to a sync, select **only the fields you need**. Syncing every field:

- Slows the sync job.
- Bloats storage and counts against row limits.
- Makes recipes harder to navigate.

A lean sync is a fast sync.

## Connection modes

Salesforce syncs support two modes:

- **Incremental sync** — only pulls records changed since the last run. Fast and efficient; ideal for large, frequently updated objects.
- **Full sync** — reloads everything. Needed after schema changes or to recover from data issues.

::tip
Enable incremental sync on high-volume objects like Opportunity and Task to keep refreshes quick. Trigger a one-time full sync whenever you add new fields.
::

## Scheduling refreshes

Data is only as useful as it is current. Schedule syncs and recipes under the **Schedule** menu:

- **Time-based** — run every hour, day, or on specific days.
- **Event-based** — run when another data sync completes, chaining your pipeline.

Match the schedule to how fresh the business actually needs the numbers to be.

## Monitoring jobs

The **Monitor** tab in Data Manager shows every run, its duration, row counts, and any errors. Check it after your first schedule to confirm the pipeline runs cleanly — then move on to shaping the data.
