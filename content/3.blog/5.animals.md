The Salesforce CRM Analytics (formerly Einstein Analytics / Tableau CRM / Wave) ecosystem has grown because of one thing: **people who share what they learn**.  

From bindings and SAQL deep dives to exam guides and project war stories, these bloggers turn a confusing product into something you can actually ship in production.

Here are **10 CRM Analytics bloggers and blogs** you should know if you’re serious about dashboards, dataflows, recipes, and real-world analytics.

## 1. Paulina Rueda – Einstein Blogger

::picture-and-text
---
card: true
---
**Why follow Paulina?**  
Paulina is the author of **Einstein Blogger**, a site dedicated almost entirely to CRM Analytics. She writes the kind of posts you want when you’re stuck at 1 a.m. with a broken dashboard: practical, screenshot-heavy, and focused on real use cases.

You’ll find content on:

- Multi-level account hierarchies and how to visualize them cleanly in CRM Analytics
- Data prep patterns for complex customer journeys
- Smart use of **CASE** statements to create flags, reorder fields, and simplify logic
- Features she “wishes she’d learned earlier” so you don’t have to suffer first

If you want a **consultant-level mental model** for CRM Analytics, this blog is a goldmine.

## 2. Rikke Hovgaard – Salesforce Blogger / Tell Your Data Story

::picture-and-text
---
card: true
---
**Why follow Rikke?**  
If you’ve ever Googled *compare tables* or *bindings* in Einstein / CRM Analytics, you’ve already seen Rikke’s work. Her posts are basically **unofficial documentation** for half the advanced features in the product.

On her blogs you’ll see:

- The iconic **“Demystifying Bindings”** series  
- Deep dives into **compare tables**, including rolling aggregations and “fun with flags”
- Practical deployment patterns, data catalog tools, and Data Cloud + Analytics content
- A constant focus on explaining *why* a technique works, not just *how* to click it

Think of Rikke as the **core textbook** for intermediate → advanced CRM Analytics builders.

## 3. Kristi Brown – Force For Fun

::picture-and-text
---
card: true
---
**Why follow Kristi?**  
Kristi’s blog, **Force For Fun**, is a Salesforce fan blog with a big soft spot for Analytics. Her legendary series **“SAQL as a Second Language”** is one of the clearest ways to learn SAQL without crying.

You’ll find:

- The *anatomy* of a SAQL query explained in human language  
- Posts on `group`, `cogroup`, `union`, and interactions between widgets  
- Thoughtful examples of how Analytics fits into real business scenarios  
- That perfect balance of **nerdy + approachable** writing

If you’re staring at SAQL and thinking “why is this like this?”, Kristi is your translator.

## Final Thoughts

The official docs and Trailhead trails are great – but the **real magic** of CRM Analytics lives in the community:  

- People who blow up their sandboxes so you don’t have to.  
- People who publish bindings fixes at midnight.  
- People who turn SAQL errors into blog posts instead of trauma.

Follow a few of these bloggers, subscribe to their feeds, and sprinkle their patterns into your own org. Your future dashboards — and your future self — will thank you.
