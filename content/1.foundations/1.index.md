# What Is CRM Analytics?

CRM Analytics (formerly Tableau CRM and Einstein Analytics) is Salesforce's native analytics platform. It lets you bring data in, shape it into fast in-memory datasets, explore it with lenses, build interactive dashboards, and layer on predictive models with Einstein Discovery — all inside Salesforce.

::note
Throughout this course we use **CRM Analytics** and **CRMA** interchangeably. You may still see the older names "Tableau CRM" or "Einstein Analytics" in some orgs and documentation.
::

## Why a separate analytics engine?

Salesforce reports and dashboards are great for operational reporting on a single object's data. They start to strain when you need to:

- Blend data from many objects, or from outside Salesforce.
- Query millions of rows with sub-second response times.
- Build guided, interactive experiences with faceting and drill-downs.
- Run predictions and surface "what to do next" recommendations.

CRM Analytics is purpose-built for those jobs. It stores data in a columnar, compressed, in-memory format that makes large-scale aggregation fast.

## The core building blocks

Five concepts carry you through everything else:

- **Connections & syncs** — how external and Salesforce data lands in the platform.
- **Recipes & dataflows** — the ETL tools that clean, join, and transform raw data.
- **Datasets** — the optimized, queryable tables that power every chart.
- **Lenses & dashboards** — exploration and the polished, interactive UI.
- **Einstein Discovery** — automated, explainable machine learning on your datasets.

::tip
Each of the five modules in this academy maps to one of these areas. By the end you will have touched the whole pipeline, from raw data to deployed prediction.
::

## How data flows through the platform

The mental model is a simple pipeline:

1. **Connect** to a source (Salesforce objects, CSV, external databases).
2. **Sync** the raw data into the platform on a schedule.
3. **Prep** it with a recipe to produce clean, analysis-ready datasets.
4. **Explore** datasets with lenses and assemble them into dashboards.
5. **Predict** outcomes with Einstein Discovery and write results back to Salesforce.

Keeping this flow in mind makes every feature easier to place.

## Who uses CRM Analytics?

- **Analysts** build datasets, dashboards, and SAQL queries.
- **Admins** manage licensing, permissions, and data security.
- **Business users** consume dashboards and act on Einstein predictions.
- **Consultants** design end-to-end analytics solutions for customers.
