# Touring Analytics Studio

Analytics Studio is the workspace where you build and manage every CRM Analytics asset. This tour shows you where each thing lives so the rest of the course feels familiar.

## Apps: how assets are organized

Everything in Studio lives inside an **app** — a container that groups related dashboards, lenses, and datasets, and controls who can see them.

- Create an app with **Create → App**.
- Share it from the app's **Share** menu using Viewer, Editor, or Manager roles.
- Use one app per project or business area to keep sharing simple.

::note
Sharing happens at the **app** level, not per dashboard. Plan your app structure around who should see what.
::

## The Browse experience

The left navigation is your home base:

- **Browse / Home** — recent and favorited assets.
- **Datasets** — every dataset you can query.
- **Recipes** — your data prep pipelines.
- **Apps** — the containers above.

A global search bar at the top jumps you to any asset by name.

## Datasets at a glance

Click any dataset to see its **fields**, row count, last refresh time, and security settings. From here you can:

- **Explore** it to open a new lens.
- Edit **field attributes** like labels and formatting.
- Review the **security predicate** controlling row-level access.

## Recipes and the Data Manager

The **Data Manager** (a separate area you reach from Studio) is where data operations live:

- **Connections** to Salesforce and external sources.
- **Syncs** that pull raw data in on a schedule.
- **Recipes** that transform that data into datasets.
- **Monitor** tab to watch jobs succeed or fail.

You will spend most of Module 2 here.

## Creating your first lens

To prove everything works end to end:

1. Open any dataset and click **Explore**.
2. CRM Analytics opens a **lens** showing a count of rows.
3. Add a grouping (for example, by a status field) to see your first chart.

That single click is the foundation of all exploration — and the starting point for the next module.
