# Exploring with Lenses

A **lens** is a single saved exploration of one dataset. Lenses are where analysis begins: you ask questions visually, and the answers become the building blocks of dashboards.

## What a lens is

Open any dataset and click **Explore** to create a lens. A lens always has:

- **One dataset** as its source.
- A **measure** (what you're counting or summing).
- Zero or more **groupings** (how you slice it).
- Optional **filters**.

Behind the scenes, every lens is just a SAQL query the UI builds for you.

## Choosing a measure

The measure answers "how much?". Start by switching from the default `Count of Rows` to something meaningful:

- `Sum of Amount` for revenue.
- `Average of Score` for quality.
- `Unique of AccountId` for distinct customer counts.

::note
You can show multiple measures at once — for example revenue and deal count side by side — to compare volume against value.
::

## Grouping to slice data

Add a **bar** (grouping) to break the measure down:

- Group by **Stage** to see pipeline by phase.
- Group by **Region**, then **Stage**, for a stacked view.
- Group by a **date** part to see a trend over time.

Each grouping you add becomes another dimension in the chart.

## Picking the right chart

The chart type should match the question:

- **Bar / column** — compare categories.
- **Line** — show change over time.
- **Donut / pie** — part-to-whole (use sparingly).
- **Scatter** — relationship between two measures.
- **Values / pivot table** — exact numbers.

::tip
Start in the default chart, get the grouping right, then switch chart types. The data shape carries over, so you can audition visuals quickly.
::

## Filtering in a lens

Add filters to focus the exploration:

- Dimension filters (Stage is "Closed Won").
- Measure filters (Amount greater than 10,000).
- Date filters (Close Date in current quarter).

Filters here become the starting point for dashboard-level filtering later.

## Saving and reusing

Save a lens into an app to reuse it, or click **Clip to Dashboard** to drop it straight onto a dashboard canvas. Most analysts explore freely in lenses, then assemble the useful ones into a dashboard — which is exactly what the next lesson covers.
