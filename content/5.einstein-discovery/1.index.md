# Predictive Analytics Intro

**Einstein Discovery** brings machine learning to CRM Analytics without code. It analyzes a dataset, explains what drives an outcome, and builds models that predict and prescribe — all in a guided interface.

## Descriptive vs. predictive vs. prescriptive

Einstein Discovery spans three kinds of insight:

- **Descriptive** — what happened (your dashboards already do this).
- **Predictive** — what is likely to happen (will this deal close?).
- **Prescriptive** — what to do about it (which lever most improves the odds?).

Discovery's value is moving you from the first to the third.

## What is a story?

A **story** is Einstein's automated analysis of an outcome variable in a dataset. You point it at a column — say, `Won` (true/false) or `Revenue` — and it:

- Finds the factors most correlated with the outcome.
- Quantifies each factor's impact.
- Surfaces interactions you might have missed.

::note
A story is generated from a **dataset**, so all your Module 2 data-prep work directly determines the quality of the model. Clean inputs, better insights.
::

## Outcome types

The outcome you choose sets the model type:

- **Binary** (Won / Lost) → classification, returns a probability.
- **Numeric** (Revenue, Days to Close) → regression, returns a value.
- **Categorical** (multi-class) → predicts the most likely class.

## Good use cases

Einstein Discovery shines when you have history and a clear outcome:

- **Win/loss prediction** on opportunities.
- **Churn risk** scoring on accounts.
- **Case escalation** likelihood for support.
- **Cycle time** estimation for fulfillment.

## What makes a good dataset

For trustworthy models, aim for:

- **Enough rows** — typically a few hundred examples of each outcome, more is better.
- **Relevant columns** — fields plausibly related to the outcome.
- **No leakage** — exclude fields that are only known *after* the outcome (a classic mistake).

::warning
**Data leakage** is the number one cause of "too good to be true" models. If a column like `CloseDate` or `Stage = Closed Won` reveals the answer, the model learns nothing useful. Remove post-outcome fields before training.
::
