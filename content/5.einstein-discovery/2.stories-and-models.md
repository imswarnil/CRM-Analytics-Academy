# Stories & Models

This lesson walks the core Einstein Discovery workflow: from a dataset to a story you can read, to a model you can trust and deploy.

## Creating a story

From a dataset, choose **Create Story** and configure:

1. **Outcome** — the column to predict (e.g. `Won`).
2. **Goal** — whether to maximize or minimize it.
3. **Story type** — *Insights only* or *Insights + Predictions*.
4. **Columns** — which fields the model may consider.

Einstein then analyzes the data and builds the story automatically.

## Reading the insights

A finished story has several tabs of explanation:

- **What Happened** — descriptive breakdowns of the outcome.
- **Why It Happened** — the factors driving it, ranked by impact.
- **Predictions** — expected outcome for given conditions.
- **Improvements** — prescriptive suggestions to move the metric.

::tip
Start in **Why It Happened**. The top factors tell you whether the model is learning something sensible — or whether a leaky column is dominating and needs removing.
::

## Evaluating model quality

Before trusting a model, check its metrics:

- **For classification** — accuracy, and more importantly the area under the ROC curve.
- **For regression** — R² and mean absolute error.
- **Model fit warnings** — Einstein flags overfitting and weak predictors.

A model that's accurate *and* explainable beats a black box every time.

## Iterating on the model

Models rarely ship on the first pass. Improve them by:

- **Removing leaky or irrelevant columns.**
- **Adding engineered features** in your recipe (ratios, buckets, recency).
- **Balancing** outcomes if one class is rare.
- **Re-running** the story and comparing metrics.

::note
Feature engineering in the recipe usually beats algorithm tweaking. The model is only as smart as the columns you feed it.
::

## Turning a story into a model

When the story looks good, click **Deploy Model**. You'll:

1. Name the model and review its predictors.
2. Map model fields to the Salesforce object that will be scored.
3. Configure **prediction settings** and any actionable variables.

The result is a live model ready to score records.

## Model lifecycle

Deployed models aren't static. Plan to:

- **Monitor** prediction quality over time as data drifts.
- **Refresh** the training dataset and redeploy periodically.
- **Version** models so you can roll back if a new one underperforms.

With a deployed model in hand, the final lesson shows how to surface those predictions where users work.
