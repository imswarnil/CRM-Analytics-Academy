interview:
  - q: "What is CRM Analytics?"
    a: "CRM Analytics is Salesforce's native analytics platform (formerly Tableau CRM, Einstein Analytics, and Wave) for building datasets, interactive dashboards, and AI-driven insights directly inside Salesforce."
  - q: "Does CRM Analytics replace standard Salesforce reports and dashboards?"
    a: "No, it complements them. Reports give an operational, live view of the transactional system of record, while CRM Analytics adds fast historical analysis and predictive, augmented insight."
  - q: "What makes a predictive score like '77' actionable for an end user?"
    a: "The reasons, explanations, and recommended actions attached to it, which tell the user why the score is what it is and what to do next."
  - q: "How does data get into CRM Analytics?"
    a: "Through the native Salesforce connector, connectors to other clouds and databases, CSV upload, or ETL/API ingestion, with data ultimately landing in datasets."
  - q: "What is the difference between a live connection and replicated data?"
    a: "A live connection (Direct Data) queries the external source such as Snowflake in real time, while replicated data is copied into a dataset for fast local querying."
  - q: "Why does CRM Analytics run on dedicated, virtualized compute?"
    a: "So heavy analytical queries run separately from the transactional database, keeping day-to-day CRM operations fast even at high data volumes."
  - q: "What is Data Sync (Connect) used for?"
    a: "It is a staging layer that brings source data in as small datasets, and for the local Salesforce connection it supports incremental refresh and filters."
  - q: "What is the difference between a Dataflow and a Recipe?"
    a: "The Dataflow is the legacy transformation editor, powerful but less user-friendly, while Recipes (Data Prep) is the modern drag-and-drop tool moving toward parity with it. Both produce datasets."
  - q: "Why are datasets called the platform's 'secret sauce'?"
    a: "Datasets are optimized columnar storage that let CRM Analytics query very high data volumes extremely fast, which is what makes dashboards and filtering feel instant."
  - q: "Explain the difference between a lens, a dashboard, and an app."
    a: "A lens is quick self-service exploration of one dataset, a dashboard is a curated multi-widget analytics view, and an app is a folder that groups dashboards, lenses, datasets, and stories while controlling access and sharing."
  - q: "What are Einstein Discovery stories and models?"
    a: "A story is the set of auto-generated insights (top predictors, influencers, explanations) on a dataset, and each story is backed by a model that can be deployed to score records with a score, explanation, and recommended action."
  - q: "What are the six pillars of CRM Analytics adoption?"
    a: "Intelligence, Collaboration, Actionability, Self-Service, Embedding, and Smart Design. Together they turn analytics from a report people ignore into a tool they use every day."
